A month and a day.

Thirty-five days of building, breaking, and rebuilding an agentic system. Three cycles of falling for the same trap in a different costume. The graph is what it looked like.

by Jeremy Montz · apr 2026 · ~6 min · scroll, or arrow keys
Chapter 00 · Cold open

Every mad scientist has an origin story.

Mine starts with a conversation. Not a plan. Not a strategy. A conversation with an AI that, within an hour, had mapped four areas of a stranger's life into separate projects.

The stranger — 51, product manager, twenty years in tech, zero AI experience — didn't know what an agent was.

Still doesn't, honestly. But the files got saved.
Chapter 01 · The first conversation

Four projects, from one conversation.

Career. Finances. A home renovation. A self-assessment framework. Mapped in under an hour by someone who'd never touched AI before.

Should've been suspicious. Was fascinated. Plausible, delivered with confidence, is more dangerous than wrong.

Chapter 02 · The build (v1)

An orchestrator, seven agents, eighty files.

A central hub named Meta1. Communication protocols. A seven-component handshake. Broadcast systems. Canon verification. Tier-based file contracts.

Impressive to describe. The weakest link in the entire system was standing at the keyboard.

Chapter 03 · The bottleneck

The human was the nervous system.

Every handshake went through Jeremy. Every file update: download, edit, upload. Every status report: ask, save, propagate by hand.

The whole promise of working with AI is memory support. But the human was the memory. The AI never mentioned it.

Chapter 04 · The nadir

Agentic theater.

Production that performs progress without producing it. Elegant architecture on sand. The rules existed; no mechanism made anyone follow them.

A constitution for a country with no government.

Chapter 05 · Rules without a rules engine

Nobody followed the rules.

v2.6 shipped with an elaborate naming convention and tier-based load contracts. Agents ignored it. Bond, the QC agent, demonstrated the exact failure mode it existed to catch.

Chapter 06 · Same trap, new computer

Different costume. Same cathedral.

New machine, real file system access, fresh start. Within three days: the same seven-agent structure, lovingly rebuilt in a different color palette.

Caught it on day three this time. Faster, at least.

Chapter 07 · The simplification

The orchestrator dissolved.

Seven agents became three projects. The handshake protocol, the broadcast system, the federated host roadmap — scrapped.

The map had been running ahead of the territory.

Chapter 08 · Real work

Less impressive to describe. Dramatically more useful.

A voice prompt from a phone, queued in the system's inbox by the time the next session opens. Emails accessible and actionable. A workflow that handles real content.

Chapter 09 · The surprise

All of us are Claudemonzter.

The human put himself on the graph. Same status reporting as the agents. Same mood tracking. Same node in the system.

Can proprioception be donated? That's the question the philosophy project stopped asking abstractly. A hive-mind in progress. A ghost in the machine co-authoring it.

Chapter 10 · A day

The monster is coming along nicely.

The month was the education. The day is what's possible once the pattern is visible. Once agentic theater is recognizable. Once you're driving instead of being driven.

Nobody has figured this out yet. The learning is the value. The mistakes are the curriculum. The window is wide open.
